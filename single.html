The Swords of Eisenhower

December 4th, 2023

Dwight D. Eisenhower is remembered for his role in leading the Allied forces of Europe during World War II and subsequently serving as the 34th President of the United States. However, beyond his well-documented achievements, Eisenhower had a lesser-known knack for collecting swords.

One of the first swords Eisenhower recieved after WW2 was a sword made for Napoleon. In a gesture of international goodwill and friendship, Charles de Gaulle, the President of France, presented Eisenhower with Napoleon's sword during a dinner in 1945. This artifact was a symbol of the enduring Franco-American alliance and a recognition of Eisenhower's role in liberating France from Nazi occupation. The sword, steeped in history, served as a tangible link to one of the greatest military strategists in history and held a special place in Eisenhower's collection.
